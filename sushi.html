Salmon sushi recipe

Description

This salmon roll is a rich and indulgent uramaki-style sushi, with seasoned sushi rice on the outside wrapped around a creamy filling of salmon and cream cheese. It is finished with a drizzle of savory sauce and topped with fresh spring onion for a pop of color.

The combination of buttery salmon and tangy cream cheese makes this roll a crowd favorite. It is best served immediately after rolling, with soy sauce on the side for dipping.

Ingredients

  • 200g sushi-grade salmon, sliced
  • 100g cream cheese
  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 4 sheets nori
  • 2 spring onions, finely sliced
  • Soy sauce, to serve
  • Teriyaki or eel sauce, to drizzle

Steps

  1. Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear, then cook according to package instructions.
  2. While the rice is still warm, gently fold in the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Spread onto a tray and let cool to room temperature.
  3. Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo mat covered with cling film. Wet your hands and spread an even layer of sushi rice over the entire nori sheet.
  4. Flip the nori sheet over so the rice faces down onto the cling film.
  5. Spread a strip of cream cheese along the center of the nori, then lay the salmon slices on top.
  6. Using the bamboo mat, roll the sushi tightly away from you, applying gentle pressure to keep the shape.
  7. Using a sharp wet knife, slice the roll into 8 equal pieces with clean cuts.
  8. Arrange on a plate, drizzle with teriyaki or eel sauce, and top with sliced spring onion.
  9. Serve immediately with soy sauce on the side.
